The break-down diary

A practical tool for chronic pain management in young adults

Record - Reflect - Relieve

The break- down diary is a campaign for social good, an exploration of how to help
young people manage chronic pain. Tracking the patterns of chronic pain can help young
people manage their lives alongside the regular interferences they may face.

This project was inspired by a lack of resources for young adults who experience chronic pain. Partially for my own benefit, this project was inspired by my experience opening up an Excel spreadsheet every day for months on end to log my symptoms. This wasn’t a well-designed solution.

Symptom diaries play an important role in not only better understanding yourself and your condition, but also for advocating for yourself within the medical system. A symptom tracker can help a doctor or specialist more accurately diagnose and understand long term pain triggers.

“The more detailed information you can give a doctor when discussing an internal problem, the faster and more accurate your diagnoses and personalized treatment plan will be. Sometimes, it is easy to forget small details or your most important questions when talking with a physician.

A symptom diary organizes the details of what is going on with your body and, in turn, helps a physician make more informed decisions about what the next steps may be. Also, when noticing triggers from repetitive things noted in the diary, you may be able to make certain lifestyle adjustments as needed.”

Covington Hospital (2024, January 11). What is a Symptom Diary and Why Do You Need One? ‌
